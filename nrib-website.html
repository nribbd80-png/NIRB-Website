Who We Are

Research for Knowledge,
Nutrition for Life.

Nutrition Research & Information Bangladesh (NRIB) is a youth-led, research-oriented platform established to promote evidence-based nutrition knowledge, public health awareness, and a strong connection between scientific discovery and community impact.

"If the knowledge produced in research laboratories never reaches ordinary people, that knowledge has no real value."

Founded on 24 January 2026 by Mst. Mahfuza Akter, NRIB aims to bridge the gap between scientific research and the general public by translating complex nutrition and health concepts into simple, actionable information.

Through research, training, scientific communication, and community engagement, NRIB strives to empower individuals to make informed decisions about nutrition and healthy lifestyles.

🔬

Research

Conducting and supporting evidence-based nutrition and public health research initiatives across Bangladesh.

📚

Education

Building research capacity among students and young professionals through training, mentorship, and workshops.

📢

Awareness

Disseminating scientific findings in a simple and accessible manner to promote healthy dietary practices.

🤝

Collaboration

Fostering partnerships with academic institutions, healthcare organizations, and community stakeholders.

Our Research

Evidence That Drives
Real Change

NRIB focuses on research areas that directly impact the health and well-being of Bangladeshi communities.

Body Image

Body Image Dissatisfaction Among Bangladeshi Youth

Exploring the psychological and social factors contributing to negative body image perceptions among young people aged 16–30, with a focus on social media's role.

🗓 2026 · Ongoing
Eating Disorders

Eating Disorder Risk Among University Students

Assessing the prevalence and determinants of disordered eating attitudes and behaviors among Bangladeshi university students using validated EAT-26 tools.

🗓 2026 · Ongoing
Public Health

Nutrition Knowledge & Dietary Habits in Rural Bangladesh

Investigating nutrition literacy levels and their relationship with dietary practices in rural Bangladeshi communities to inform targeted interventions.

🗓 2026 · Planning

"To become a trusted national platform for nutrition research, education, and public health awareness — contributing to a healthier, well-informed, and nutritionally empowered Bangladesh."
